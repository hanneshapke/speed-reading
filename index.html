01 / THE BOTTLENECK

Three habits are slowing you down.

Slow reading isn't a lack of intelligence — it's a set of childhood habits your eyes never unlearned. Name them and you can train them out.

A

Subvocalization

Sounding out every word in your head. It chains your reading speed to your speaking speed — roughly 200 wpm. You don't need to "hear" a word to understand it.

B

Regression

Flicking your eyes backward to re-read. Skilled readers regress less by trusting the first pass and letting comprehension catch up.

C

Narrow fixation

Stopping on one word at a time. Each stop costs ~0.25s. Widen your span to grab 2–3 words per stop and you halve the number of stops.

02 / WIDEN THE SPAN

See in chunks, not words.

Your eye doesn't read while moving — only during the brief stops between jumps. Fewer stops means faster reading. Watch the same sentence get grouped into bigger bites.

06 / THE PRACTICE

A five-minute daily routine.

01

Warm up at speed

Run the trainer 30% faster than feels comfortable for one minute. Don't aim to comprehend — just let your eyes get used to the pace.

02

Silence the voice

Hum or count "1-2-3" softly while reading a paragraph. It occupies the speech loop so you can't subvocalize, forcing visual reading.

03

Pace a real page

Use a pen or the pacer above to sweep under each line at a steady clip. Never let it stop, and never go back.

04

Drop back and check

Read a fresh passage at a comfortable speed and write one sentence summarizing it. Comprehension is the real score.
